Ocean Services


Oxygen


It's estimated that the ocean's ecosystem contributes between 50% to 70% of the atmosphere's oxygen. These are only a small portion of the plants found in the ocean's ecosystem. There are millions of other plants in the ocean that all contribute to the oxygen in our atmosphere.

Phytoplankton

Phytoplankton are microscopic plant-like organisms. They can sometimes be found floating along the ocean's surface. These guys are a large produces of oxygen and help perpetuate the food web.

Coral Reefs

Coral reefs only cover a small portion of the ocean, but they contribute largely to oxygen production. The coral itself doesn't produce oxygen, however, they foster entire ecosystems of fish and plants, like algae.

Kelp

Kelp is another contributor to oxygen production from the ocean. They also perpetuate ecosystems around them. Unfortunately, some kelp forests are being destroyed due to poor climate conditions and species imbalances.

Food


Tuna, Salmon, Cod

These are only a few of the types of fish that can be caught in the ocean. To some, these fish can be luxurious meals, or they can also be a common grocery item that can be found in many households. To over a billion people, these fish can be a vital source of protein. Historically, some indigenous peoples in Canada and European settlers would fish along the coastlines. In the past, the ocean's fish allowed the formation of civilization, and today, they are still often eaten

Seaweed

You might already be familiar with a couple of the seaweeds that the ocean provides. Nori is a thin, sheet-like seaweed that is used in some Japanese foods like sushi and onigiri. There is also Dulse, a popular snack in some places. It's rich in minerals and a great source of protein. Astronauts can even use seaweed as a food in space. Historically, seaweed was sometimes used as a fertilizer and is still sometimes used in fertilizer in modern times because of its high mineral content.

Energy Production


Even those who have never seen the ocean before would likely know about the ocean's strong waves and currents. For centuries, we've used water to power our creations and in modern times, we've used ocean currents and tides for clean and reliable energy. When the tides are strong enough, we can harness them and create electricity. This is usually done by placing turbines in the water. As the tides move through the turbines, they rotate and create electricity much like a wind turbine would. Just one tidal powerplant can generate 600 gigawatt hours annually, or enough to power fifty thousand homes. It is speculated that we can generate up to 3800 terawatt hours annually in the future.

The ocean is vital not only for the fish in it but also us, who are on land. The amount of oxygen that it creates surmounts even all of the forests on land. The food it provides can be considered a delicacy to some but also vital nutrients to others. The energy it provides is not only clean and renewable, but it has the potential to power millions of homes. Much appreciation is due for the ocean and all that it gives us.
